Casilico Guide

The user can run Casilico using the default mode or by altering the conservation threshold or the way of identifying conserved windows. The conservation threshold determines polymorphic positions, so that, one position will be polymorphic with a major allele frequency less than the conservation threshold. Two different ways to identify conserved windows: (1) most or all positions have to be conserved and only two or fewer polymorphic positions or in other words, mismatches can occur in specific positions of the window, (2) most or all positions have to be conserved, allowing only two or fewer positions (throughout the window) to have a major allele frequency less than conservation threshold.

The results are displayed in an interactive table and graphical visualization. All results table options are defined below. A few parameters are only found in some CRISPR-Cas systems depending on the targeting mechanism of Cas enzymes. The graphical visualization displays (I) information plot, (II) spacer and crRNA self-complementarity structure, and (III) spacer dot plot.

Guide_RNA:

The designed crRNA by Casilico is the reverse complementary sequence of the genomic target region. In all cases, the crRNA is displayed 5' to 3'.

Guide_RNA_ID:

This option is crRNA identifier as shows applied type and subtype of CRISPR-Cas system, the way of identifying conserved window, and crRNA number, respectively.

Major_Allele_Frequency:

Casilico obtains allele frequency for each consensus base all over the window.

Start:

The position of the genomic target region on the consensus sequence.

Strand:

Casilico searches for potential crRNAs on both strands when a double-stranded sequence is targeted. The strand that crRNA has been located on is displayed by +/-.

Mismatch_Number:

This option shows the number of bases that have an allele frequency less than the conservation threshold.

Mismatch_Position:

The position of each mismatch is specified on the spacer sequence.

Type_Of_SNP:

This parameter displays other bases of a polymorphic position.

Conservation Efficiency:

Casilico computes conservation efficiency by getting the average of the conservatory in any position.

Local_U/A_Rich:

Target nucleotide U or A content for the potential target sites of a single sequence is calculated as the proportion of Us or As (depend on chosen Cas) in 100 nt windows upstream and downstream of spacer site called local U/A-rich. When there is a set of sequences, after MSA and obtaining allele frequency for each consensus base, Us or As frequencies is divided by frequencies of all bases in 100 nt windows upstream and downstream of spacer site to calculate local U/A-rich.

Normalized_U/A_Rich:

The local U/A-rich is normalized by the proportion of Us or As throughout the gene to create normalized U/A-rich.

Protospacer_Accessibility_Score:

Casilico gets target site nucleotides and 1000 nt upstream and downstream of them (using consensus bases for a set of sequences) to draw a graphical model of optimal secondary structure with minimum free energy (MFE) using RNAfold from the ViennaRNA package. For sequences with a length of less than 2000 nt, all bases are used for this purpose. Accordingly, protospacer accessibility score is computed as the number of CRISPR target site unpaired bases divided by the length of the target site.

Local_Accessibility_Score:

The frequency of all unpaired bases in 100 nt windows upstream and downstream of the spacer site is calculated as the local accessibility score.

Rank:

The number of ranks is related to the mismatch number as a crRNA with no mismatch get higher rank (Rank 1) and the number of rank increases when mismatches occur into the spacer.

Self_Complementarity_Free_Energy:

To anticipate detrimental interactions, self-complementarity between DR and spacer is predicted by drawing the secondary structure of candidate crRNAs with MFE using RNAfold. Intense self-complementarity between DR and spacer could abolish the natural secondary structure of DR and the absence of self-complementarity allow DR to form a normal structure. The whole free energy of a crRNA with an unnatural DR structure is reported while for crRNAs without internal interactions, the free energy of the spacer when it is connected to the DR is shown.

Off_Target:

This option indicates the number of off-target effects for each candidate crRNA in columns NO_mismatch, 1_mismatch, 2_mismatches, etc. By clicking on each column, detailed information includes the organisms and genes in which off-targets have been found is displayed.

Casilico integrates off-target effects results in a separate table named the off-targets results table.

Plot:

By Clicking on the plot option for each crRNA in the results table, a page containing information plot, spacer and crRNA self-complementarity structure, and spacer dot plot is displayed.
